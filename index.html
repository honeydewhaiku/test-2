Everything Else:

a lyric essay.

Published in Gravel magazine in February 2017, this creative nonfiction essay speaks of beautiful thinking.

I want to write again, she told me one day, sitting outside the front of her house, smoking a cigarette. The smoke drifted into the gray sky and faded like the unintelligible, inexplicable fragments of a dream upon waking. You should, I said. It was the best healing I knew of.

On Being Insane:

a memoir.

Anticipated release Fall 2017. Read the introduction.

Diagnosed with Asperger's Disorder at age seven, Elliot becomes fascinated with the Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders (DSM; the Bible of American psychiatry) and its enumeration, categorization, and systematization of innate human differences. However, Elliot's dreams of being a psychologist seem ever further out of reach until one professor takes notice of his unique abilities.

About me.



I am a PhD student in Human Development & Psychology at the University of California, Los Angeles. I'm 21 years old. My favorite color (if you were wondering) is blue, which is convenient as I consider myself a Ravenclaw. I love Pokémon; I also love strategy board games, cats, swingsets, and using italics. You may have heard that I am on the autism spectrum. Alternatively you may have heard that I am bipolar, or that I'm transgender. Fortunately, these rumors are true.
